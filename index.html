Speedster 3000

The Demeris Speedster 3000 isn't just a car — it's a statement of futuristic absurdity wrapped in glossy plastic. Towering at an incredible four meters in height and weighing a staggering five tons, this machine somehow manages to deliver a mind-bending top speed of 750 km/h—all powered by nothing more than two humble AA batteries (not included, of course).

Demeris Technology has always been less about practicality and more about spectacle.
Founded by eccentric inventor Cassian Demeris in the late 21st century, the company began as a boutique workshop crafting experimental machines that blurred the line between toys and titans.
Where other brands pursued efficiency and safety, Demeris embraced audacity — constructing vehicles that defied engineering norms, powered by unlikely energy sources, and built from materials better suited to playgrounds than racetracks.
